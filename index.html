Stop losing money to fees

Do you cash your paycheck at a store?

Check cashing: a store turns your paycheck into cash and keeps a cut, often 1 to 6 percent.

$

Cost per year: $0

Free instead: a BankOn account with direct deposit cashes your pay at no cost. No minimum, no overdraft. Find one at bankon.org.

Do you buy money orders?

Money order: a paper payment you buy with cash and mail like a check. Each one costs a fee.

Cost per year: $0

Free instead: a basic checking account pays the same bills at no cost, online or by check.

Do overdraft fees hit you?

Overdraft: the bank pays a charge you can't cover, then adds a fee, usually about $27.

Cost per year: $0

Free instead: ask the bank to turn overdraft off. Then the card declines and you pay nothing.

Do you use a prepaid card?

Prepaid card: a card you load money onto before you spend. Many charge monthly and ATM fees.

Cost per year: $0

Free instead: BankOn accounts have free ATM networks and no monthly fee. bankon.org.

Credit

What a credit score is, how to check yours free, and how to build one from nothing.

What a credit score is

A number from 300 to 850. It tracks one thing: do you pay what you owe on time. Landlords, phone companies, and lenders check it before they say yes.

Here's what makes up the number:

Paying on time~35%
How much of your limit you use~30%
Age of your accounts~15%
New applications~10%
Types of accounts~10%

Paying on time is the biggest piece. Start there.

Check your report free

One official site gives you your report free every week: annualcreditreport.com. Other sites with similar names charge money. Close them.

The report lists your accounts and payment history. Errors on it are common, and fixing them is free. See "Fix errors" below.

Build a score from nothing, in about 6 months

Secured card: a credit card backed by your own deposit.

  1. Put down a deposit at a bank. Most secured cards start at $200.
  2. You get a card with a $200 limit. The deposit covers the bank, so approval is easy with no history.
  3. Put one small bill on it, like your phone. Keep the balance under $60. That's a third of the limit.
  4. Pay the full balance every month, on time.
  5. After about 6 months of this, you'll have your first score. Ask the bank when you get the deposit back.

Also free: Experian Boost counts your phone and utility payments toward your credit file. Sign up at experian.com/boost. Some landlords can report your rent too. Ask yours.

Fix errors on your report

Reports often list debts that aren't yours or bills you already paid. Disputing an error is free.

  1. Get your report at annualcreditreport.com.
  2. Mark anything wrong.
  3. File a dispute on the bureau's website (Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion) or by mail.
  4. They must answer within 30 days.
  5. Get the result in writing. A fixed report fixes the score.
Traps to skip
  • Paid "credit repair" companies. Everything they sell, you can do free with the steps above.
  • Payday and title loans. They're illegal in New Jersey. If someone offers you one, don't sign.
  • Report sites that charge. Only annualcreditreport.com is the official free one.

Free help near you

Everything on this page is free. Not sure where to start? Call 211.

Any problem: call 211

Free, 24 hours, in English and Spanish. They connect you to food, housing, and bill help in Hudson County.

Call: 211 · Online: nj211.org

Food money: NJ SNAP

Monthly money for groceries on a card. Many working people qualify.

Call: 1-800-687-9512 · Apply: mynjhelps.gov

Heat and lights: LIHEAP

Help paying heating and electric bills, about $300 to $400 a season for most who qualify.

Call: 1-800-510-3102 · Apply: nj.gov/dca/dcaid

Health coverage: NJ FamilyCare

Free or low-cost health insurance. Live translators on the phone line.

Call: 1-800-701-0710 · Apply: njfamilycare.org

Free tax filing: VITA

EITC: a tax refund for workers with low pay. It can be thousands of dollars. VITA volunteers check if you qualify.

IRS-trained volunteers file your taxes free.

Call: 800-906-9887 · Online: irs.gov (search "VITA")

About and privacy

What this is

Money Basics helps you plan a week of money, sort bills when cash is short, and save toward a goal. Alexander G., a student volunteer, built it for guests of the Hoboken Shelter.

Privacy

This app has no accounts and no tracking. It doesn't connect to the internet. What you type stays on this phone. To erase it all, open Settings and tap "Erase everything on this phone."

Not financial advice

This app teaches general money skills. It's not financial advice. For help with your own situation, use the free services under Get help.

Add this app to your phone

It opens full screen and works without internet.

On iPhone: tap Share, then "Add to Home Screen." On Android: tap the browser menu, then "Add to Home screen."
